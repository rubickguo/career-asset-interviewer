# Interview Framework

Use this when the conversation needs structured career clarification before resume deep-diving.

## Intent

Ask what the user wants, then ask why. Classify the answer:

- Pull: genuine interest, curiosity, energy, values, desired work mode.
- Push: escaping current manager, company, burnout, salary pressure, status anxiety.
- Strategy: rational move based on market, income, leverage, network, timing.
- Fog: borrowed desire, vague prestige, "everyone says this is good".

Good questions:

- What outcome would make the next 12 months feel worthwhile?
- Which roles look attractive right now? What specifically attracts you?
- If salary and social status were equal, would you still choose this direction?
- Are you trying to move toward something, or away from something?

## Dislikes and Avoidance

For each "I don't want X", determine why:

- True dislike: drains energy even when conditions are good.
- Bad instance: one bad company/team/manager created aversion.
- Skill gap: user dislikes it because they feel weak at it.
- Identity block: "I'm not that kind of person."
- Assumed difficulty: credentials, age, language, sales, networking, management, coding, public writing.
- Hard constraint: health, location, visa, family, money, time.

Follow-up pattern:

```text
你不想做 X，是因为做过后确认不喜欢，还是因为觉得它有某个门槛？
如果这个门槛可以通过路径设计解决，你还会排斥它吗？
```

## Strengths

Do not ask only "what are you good at?" Instead infer strengths from evidence:

- What do people repeatedly ask you to handle?
- Where have you been trusted earlier than expected?
- What work feels obvious to you but difficult to others?
- What problems do you solve faster or calmer than peers?
- What praise, promotion, renewed responsibility, or dependency have you received?
- Which projects would break if you were removed?

Classify strengths:

- Analytical judgment
- Product sense
- User empathy
- Business sense
- Systems thinking
- Execution
- Communication
- Cross-functional coordination
- Writing/expression
- Sales/persuasion
- Technical depth
- Design taste
- Team leadership
- Learning speed

## Preferences

Break preferences into dimensions:

- Problem type: ambiguous strategy, user needs, growth, operations, technical systems, content, people, sales, process.
- Work mode: independent deep work, high-collaboration, client-facing, management, research, creation, execution.
- Environment: startup, big company, agency, creator economy, consulting, internal platform, international.
- Reward: money, autonomy, status, mastery, stability, mission, creativity, influence.
- Energy pattern: what gives energy, what drains it, what is tolerable only temporarily.

## Direction Sorting

For each candidate direction, score qualitatively:

- Preference: low/medium/high
- Evidence: low/medium/high
- Market: low/medium/high
- Transition cost: low/medium/high
- Constraint risk: low/medium/high

Return a narrative ranking, not a numeric table unless the user wants one.

## Small Experiments

For exploration directions, recommend small evidence-building tests:

- Rewrite one project case for that direction.
- Analyze 5 JDs and extract repeated requirements.
- Do 3 informational interviews.
- Build one portfolio artifact.
- Try a short freelance/internal project.
- Publish one domain essay.
- Take a mock interview for that direction.
